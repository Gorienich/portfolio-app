Networking & Security

FortiGate

My exposure to FortiGate comes from real connectivity and operations work: firewall behavior, VPN paths, routing decisions, gateway checks, and troubleshooting traffic flow between users, local networks, providers, and remote services.

VPN

I study VPN behavior from an infrastructure perspective: tunnel overhead, route behavior, connection drops, latency impact, MTU limitations, and how VPN conditions affect real applications such as Citrix, CRM, and VoIP.

In my Citrix troubleshooting project, VPN encapsulation overhead was connected to effective MTU limitations and intermittent application freezes.

Networking Lab

I build self-learning labs with GNS3, Packet Tracer, Wireshark, VMware, and Hyper-V to practice routing, switching, VPN, firewall logic, traffic analysis, and troubleshooting scenarios without touching production devices

After completing CCNA, my direction is deeper networking, CCNP-level practice, security fundamentals, pentesting labs, and long-term growth toward advanced network and cybersecurity expertise.

VoIP

I work with VoIP and SIP from an operational reliability perspective: softphone connectivity, SIP reachability, call-center communication stability, port checks, and the relationship between network behavior and voice quality.

Network Troubleshooting

My troubleshooting philosophy is based on understanding the full network path before making assumptions. I collect evidence using tools such as PowerShell, ping, tracert, PathPing, TCP port checks, DNS checks, logs, and MTU tests.
